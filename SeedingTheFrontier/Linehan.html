Tom Linehan

Tom Linehan was an influential organizer, community builder, and visionary educator, renowned for his foundational role in developing the Arts, Technology, and Emerging Communication (ATEC) program at the University of Texas at Dallas. Though not an artist himself, Linehan profoundly shaped the trajectory of numerous creative careers by cultivating collaborative environments and fostering interdisciplinary communities.

As a pioneering figure in digital and interactive education, Linehan brought together faculty, students, and innovators from diverse fields, including art, science, technology, and design. His commitment to transdisciplinary collaboration attracted esteemed educators, artists, and researchers, establishing ATEC as a dynamic platform for original thought, innovation, and impactful work.

Beyond his administrative achievements, Linehan was an active participant in organizations such as SIGGRAPH and served on the editorial board of Leonardo Journal at MIT Press, demonstrating his enduring dedication to the intersection of arts and sciences. His unique vision of academia as a catalyst for genuine innovation inspired the creation of influential groups like the ArtSciLab.
