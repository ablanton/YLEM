Roman Verostko
(1929 - 2024)

Roman Verostko, was a leading visionary at the intersection of Art and Technology. He transformed the landscape of digital creativity through his pioneering work in algorithmic art. During the years (particularly between 1963 and 1968) when he was a Benedictine monk, Verostko struggled with what he termed "visual oppositions that complemented and created each other in a visual dialectic. The visual dialectic in my artwork echoed my personal struggle to reconcile the believable and the unbelievable in my spiritual life," (Verostko, 2015). By 1967, Verostko had begun experimenting with emerging new media with a focus on electronics. This interest led him to create electronically synchronized audio-visual programs featuring projection of evocative texts with drawings & images and soundtracks.

Roman Verostko

Verostko's transformation into an 'artist programmer' represents a unique synthesis of his deep studies into language, philosophy, theology and spirituality – all of which provided a profound philosophical dimension to his art. He was able to seamlessly blend the precision of writing code with the fusion of spiritual inquiry and technological innovation that projected him into the forefront of computer-generated art, as he perfected his own custom-built software "Hodos". Over a decade Verostko converted his studio into what he called an "electronic scriptorium", complete with computers and drawing machines known as "pen plotters" that wielded oriental brushes. The algorithms that Verostko created guided his 'pen plotters' to generate drawings unlike anything he had ever seen. Thanks to the computer, he crafted a pathway that opened up new frontiers of form that uniquely informed his experimental process of writing computer code for creative purposes.

Verostko's legacy as a co-founder of the Algorists helped create a shared identity for artists who used their own algorithms to generate their art, distinguishing their practice from those who used pre-existing software – thus solidifying the importance of code as a creative medium in its own right. Verostko's contributions to the field of generative art have left an indelible mark on the art World. His distinctive body of work remains a unique guiding force for future artists, encouraging them to delve into the limitless potential of computer-generated creativity. This reinforces Verostko's standing as a true giant at the crossroads of programming and artistic expression.

Exe Lungshan
